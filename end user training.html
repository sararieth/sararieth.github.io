END USER TRAINING

Client: Microsoft Education

Project Overview

This end user education portal tailored to K-12 educators has remained a popular, active resource since its initial launch in 2015. During the pandemic, site visits surged, and we were given the opportunity to refresh and add new content to better support educators as they managed their classrooms remotely. As of 2025, this portal continues to draw daily learners, with total lifetime views surpassing 3 million.

View the live demo hub: Microsoft OneNote for Education

Project Background

The exponential adoption of Microsoft OneNote for use in public and private classrooms across North America created a need for educator training resources on a large scale. To help teachers and administrators effectively leverage OneNote and inclusive classroom tools, we were tasked by the OneNote in Education principal product manager to plan and build learning modules tailored to new and novice users.

Planning and Collaboration

In collaboration with our CEO, creative director, technical program manager, and content manager, I worked to outline demo flows and determine an effective style for the project.

My Key Individual Contributions to the Project
  • Researched feature capabilities
  • Outlined demo flows based on client direction and internal user testing of the first-run product experience for end users
  • End-to-end production
    • Provisioned and configured demo environments
    • Designed demo environment content to resonate with educators (e.g. fictitious user emails, classroom assignments, persona interactions)
    • Captured demo screenshot flows
    • Wrote and peer-edited demo scripts
    • Managed audio vendor
    • Produced interactive content (e.g. image editing and cleanup, audio pacing, adding interactive user prompts)
