SALES ENABLEMENT

Client: Microsoft Industry Solutions

Project Overview

These 24 (and counting) day-in-the-life product tours featuring Microsoft 365 Copilot and AI agents, were created to help sales teams across the government, banking, capital markets, and insurance industries learn about and present new product features.

Individual demos are hosted on easy-to-navigate industry hubs, and serve a dual purpose:

Sales training: the demos enable sales teams to quickly explore new product features and learn how best to position them in sales presentations
Presentation assets: sales teams share the demos on social media and directly with customers in one-on-one presentations. The format allows them to walk customers through product feature capabilities in what feels like a live environment—without worrying about where to click, what to say, or running into errors or connectivity issues

View the live demo hubs for each industry here: Government, Banking, Capital Markets, Insurance

Project Background

Microsoft Copilot feature development was moving fast, and the technical documentation, training, and brand assets were lagging behind. Sales teams and general users were having a difficult time keeping track of new features and how to use them effectively. We were contracted by a team of industry product owners and developers at Microsoft Industry Solutions to research key features and build easy-to-navigate sales training and presentation content for industry-specific Copilot capabilities.

Project Timelines

Each of these 24 individual demos had a turnaround time of approximately two weeks—from initial client request to final delivery.

My Role in the Process

At the kick-off of each new demo, I worked with client stakeholders and our technical program manager to outline step-by-step demo flows. My main role at this stage was to provide feedback and edits on demo flows and screen captures.

Once a demo flow was established and raw screenshots were captured, I picked up the project and owned it from this stage to completion.

Here’s what I contributed as the sole writer, designer, and producer for all 24 demos:

  • Researched feature capabilities, real-world use case scenarios, and Microsoft's current brand positioning
  • Developed personas and crafted storylines
  • End-to-end production
    • Wrote demo scripts
    • Designed demo environment content to resonate with each industry vertical (e.g. intro graphics, mobile layouts, fictitious work content, persona interactions)
    • Generated AI audio voiceover
    • Produced interactive content (e.g. image editing and cleanup, audio pacing, adding interactive user prompts)
    • Designed demo hub pages for hosting
